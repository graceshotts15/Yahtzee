The Rules of Yahtzee

There are 5 dice in a yahtzee game, and 13 (or 14) different options to be filled in on your score card. You are able to roll the dice at most of 3 times per turn. You want to fill in the score card with everything except 0's as your points. You are allowed to enter a 0 anywhere, but you aren't able to change it afterwards. The goal is to complete the score card. Each spot has a different way to get those points. In the list below, it will show each way to get the points for that certain option on the score card.

Scoring

  • One's: Select this option to score the total of 1's you have rolled: (0-5).
  • Two's: Select this option to score the total of 2's you have rolled: (0-10).
  • Three's: Select this option to score the total of 3's you have rolled: (0-15).
  • Four's: Select this option to score the total of 4's you have rolled: (0-20).
  • Five's: Select this option to score the total of 5's you have rolled: (0-25).
  • Sixes: Select this option to score the total of 6's you have rolled: (0-30).
  • 3 of a Kind: You have to get 3 of the same number. (EX: 5, 5, 3, 2, 5)
  • 4 of a Kind: You have to get 4 of the same number. (EX: 5, 5, 5, 2, 5)
  • Full House: You have to get 3 of one number, and two of another. (EX: 4, 4, 4, 2, 2)
  • Small Straight: You have to get at least 4 numbers in a row. (EX: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6)
  • Large Straight: You have to get at least 5 numbers in a row. (EX: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5)
  • Yahtzee: You have to get 5 of a kind, all the same number. (EX: 1, 1, 1, 1, 1)
  • Chance: You are able to put anything in this space. Its a free space basically.
  • Yahtzee Bonus: You can only get points in this spot if you get a second yahtzee, the first yahtzee spot has to be filled in already.
